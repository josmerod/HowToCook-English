# Old Style Guo Bao Rou (Lao Shi Guo Bao Rou)

Guo Bao Rou is a famous dish in Northeast China, founded by Zheng Xingwen, a chef in Harbin Daotai Mansion during Guangxu period. The acidity of Old Style Guo Bao Rou comes from white vinegar juice, tasting sweet and sour and crispy.

Estimated Cooking Difficulty: ★★★★

## Essential Ingredients and Tools

- Pork Loin (Tong Ji Rou)
- Big Scallion
- Ginger
- Garlic
- Carrot (Optional)
- Coriander
- White Vinegar (Suggest to use 9 degree vinegar, only this way will have distinctive vinegar fragrance of Old Style Guo Bao Rou)
- White Sugar
- Cooking Wine
- Salt
- MSG
- Potato Starch
- All Purpose Flour (Zhong Jin Mian Fen)
- Baking Soda
- White Roasted Sesame Seeds (Optional)
- Cooking Oil

## Calculations

Per portion (About 2 person portion):

- Pork Loin 300g
- Big Scallion 50g
- Ginger 30g
- Garlic 3-4 cloves
- Carrot 10g (Optional)
- Coriander 10g
- White Roasted Sesame Seeds 5g (Optional)
- White Vinegar 40g
- White Sugar 40g
- Cooking Wine 20ml
- Salt 8g
- MSG 5g
- Rice Vinegar 5ml (Optional)
- Potato Starch 210g
- All Purpose Flour 70g
- Baking Soda 5g
- Cooking Oil 1000ml (For frying)

## Operation

1. **Process Pork**:
   - Cut Pork Loin into uniform meat slices with thickness of 8mm, remove white fascia.
   - Rinse meat slices with clear water, remove blood water.
   - Add Baking Soda 5g, grab evenly, stand for 5 minutes.
   - Rinse with clear water 1-2 times, remove excessive Baking Soda.

2. **Marinate Meat Slices**:
   - Add Salt 4g, Cooking Wine 5ml into meat slices, mix well, marinate for 15 minutes.

3. **Prepare Batter**:
   - **Method 1**:
     - Add 200ml clear water to 100g Potato Starch, stir well, stand for 20 minutes.
     - Pour out upper 2/3 clear water, keep bottom starch paste, stir well until yogurt-like.
   - **Method 2 (Recommended)**:
     - Mix Potato Starch 210g and All Purpose Flour 70g.
     - Add clear water in small amounts multiple times, stir until yogurt-like, can pull silk when lifted, paste can pile up in basin.
     - Add Cooking Oil 10ml, Baking Soda 1g, stir evenly.

4. **Coat Batter**:
   - Put marinated meat slices into paste, mix well, make every slice coated with paste evenly.

5. **Mix Sweet and Sour Juice**:
   - Mix White Sugar 40g, White Vinegar 40g, Salt 4g, MSG 5g, Rice Vinegar 5ml (Optional), stir evenly, set aside.

6. **Prepare Side Dishes**:
   - Cut Scallion, Ginger, Carrot into shreds, cut Coriander into segments, cut Garlic into slices.

7. **Fry Meat Slices**:
   - **First Frying**:
     - Add cooking oil to pot, heat to 150℃ (50% hot).
     - Put meat slices into oil one by one, fry until surface fixed shape, color light yellow, about 30 seconds, ladle out set aside.
   - **Second Frying**:
     - Oil temperature rises to 170℃ (60% hot).
     - Put all meat slices into oil, fry until shell golden yellow and crispy, about 1 minute, ladle out set aside.
   - **Third Frying (Optional)**:
     - Oil temperature rises to 200℃ (70% hot).
     - Re-fry meat slices quickly 10-20 seconds, enhance crispiness, ladle out drain oil.

8. **Stir-fry**:
   - Leave 20ml base oil in pot, heat then put in Ginger shreds, Garlic slices, fry aromatic 10 seconds.
   - Pour in mixed Sweet and Sour Juice, high heat heat to boil, simmer until soup juice sticky, about 30-60 seconds.
   - Put in fried meat slices, Scallion shreds, Carrot shreds, Coriander segments, quickly stir-fry 3 times, make soup juice uniformly coat meat slices.

9. **Serve**:
   - Dish Guo Bao Rou into plate, sprinkle White Roasted Sesame Seeds (Optional), ready to serve.

## Additional Content

- **Precautions**:
  1. Meat slice thickness should be 8mm, too thick or too thin will affect texture.
  2. Control oil temperature when frying, avoid shell too dark or not crispy.
  3. When simmering sweet and sour juice, pay attention to simmer until sticky but not clumping, avoid turning into caramel.
